Paul

Paul the Apostle, formerly Saul of Tarsus, was a Pharisee who became a key figure in Christianity after a dramatic encounter with the risen Jesus on the road to Damascus. Once a persecutor of Christians, he dedicated his life to spreading the gospel and establishing churches across the Roman Empire. Paul wrote many letters that form much of the New Testament, emphasizing salvation by grace through faith. He was martyred in Rome and is remembered as a missionary, theologian, and pillar of the early Church.
